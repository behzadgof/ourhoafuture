Why Advanced Roofing Appears With Two Different Prices

Two different document formats show two different totals

Residents may notice that Advanced Roofing appears with two very different totals depending on which document is viewed.

In the Engineer Bid Comparison Sheet

Advanced Roofing appears at approximately: $999,153

In the Contractor Proposal

  • $1,719,270 (Soprema roofing system with bond)
  • $1,778,603 (TPO roofing system with bond)

Different totals can appear when documents are presented in different formats. Possible reasons include:

  • different cost categories being grouped together
  • bond costs being included or excluded
  • optional work or alternates being treated differently
  • mobilization or general conditions being structured differently

Because these documents present pricing in different formats, the totals may not be directly comparable without additional explanation.

Providing a reconciliation between the engineer’s bid comparison sheet and the contractor’s detailed proposal could help residents understand how the $999K figure was calculated, how it relates to the $1.7M proposal, and which totals are being used for budgeting and decision making.

Mobilization typically refers to the costs required to start a construction project. These may include:

  • equipment delivery
  • dumpsters and lifts
  • staging areas
  • temporary protection
  • project setup logistics
  • supervision and safety preparation

Contractors sometimes structure these costs differently depending on their proposal format.

Typical Construction Project Process

Where these documents appear to fit in a normal project workflow
  1. The engineer prepares project specifications
  2. Contractors submit proposals (bids)
  3. The engineer prepares a bid comparison sheet
  4. The board selects a contractor
  5. A construction contract is negotiated and signed
  6. Work is scheduled and begins

The documents currently posted appear to correspond to the proposal and bid comparison stage of this process.
