# Roof Replacement Analysis for `garage-project.html`

## Purpose

This document provides a neutral, owner-facing summary of the roof replacement pricing documents posted for Turnberry on the Green. Its purpose is to help present the published bid information in a clear and careful way, while distinguishing between:

- **Engineer bid-tab values**
- **Detailed contractor proposal values**
- **Base totals**
- **Bonded totals**
- **Totals including alternates**

This draft is designed to reduce apples-to-oranges comparisons and keep the website language informational.

1. **Summary cards**

3. **Expandable detail section**

4. **Clarification questions**  
   Frame unresolved issues as questions for clarification rather than conclusions.

## Important Comparison Rules

Before presenting any numbers together, label them by basis:

- **Bid Tab** = one-page engineer comparison sheet
- **Detailed Proposal** = contractor’s own multi-page proposal
- **Base Total** = contractor total before bond, electrical, or alternates where applicable
- **Bonded Total** = total after bond fee
- **Total with Additions** = includes electrical and/or alternates where specifically stated

### Important note
Not all posted totals are directly comparable. Some numbers come from a bid-tab summary and others come from detailed proposals that may use different quantity assumptions, scope organization, or cost allocation methods.

## What “Mobilization” Usually Means

In construction, **mobilization** generally refers to the cost of setting up and starting the project. Depending on the contract format, it may include some or all of the following:

- delivery of equipment and materials
- staging, dumpsters, lifts, and temporary protections
- temporary fencing, storage, and signage
- startup logistics and coordination
- initial site supervision and safety setup

Because contractors sometimes organize costs differently, mobilization line items are not always directly comparable unless the bid instructions required the same definition.

## Why the Prices May Differ

The website should frame this as a list of possible explanations, not conclusions.

### Possible reasons the totals differ
1. **Different document basis**  
   One-page engineer bid-tab sheets and multi-page contractor proposals may not use the same presentation format.

2. **Different scope treatment**  
   Some documents may organize flat-roof work, hip-roof work, flashing, drainage, electrical, or alternates differently.

3. **Different quantity assumptions**  
   Some posted documents appear to use different square footage or quantity takeoffs.

4. **Different cost allocation methods**  
   One contractor may load more cost into mobilization, general conditions, or overhead than another.

5. **Different inclusion of bond, electrical, or alternates**  
   Some totals are base figures; others include bond or additional items.

### Suggested neutral website sentence
> The posted totals may differ because the documents do not all appear to use the same pricing basis or cost allocation method. A line-by-line reconciliation would help owners determine which figures are directly comparable.
