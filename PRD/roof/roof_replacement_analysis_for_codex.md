## What Is Mobilization?

In construction, **mobilization** usually means the cost of getting the job started and setting up the work site, such as:

- equipment delivery
- lifts, staging, dumpsters, and temporary fencing
- setup of site logistics
- initial supervision and safety setup
- temporary storage, signage, and project startup costs

For a project of this size, mobilization is usually a relatively limited percentage of the total cost.

### Why mobilization is a major issue here
On the ACG engineer comparison sheet, Advanced Roofing shows a mobilization amount of:

- **$548,665.84**

That is extremely large compared with the other bidders:

- **CARE:** $15,000
- **JK Inc:** $41,500

This raises an obvious question:

> Why is Advanced Roofing’s mobilization more than half a million dollars when the others are only in the tens of thousands?

That number appears unusually high for ordinary mobilization alone and may include general conditions, overhead, supervision, or other bundled costs. At minimum, it should be clearly explained.

## Issues and Red Flags in the Contract Documents

### 1. Advanced Roofing appears with two very different prices
The biggest issue is that Advanced Roofing is shown at about **$999K** in the engineer bid tab, but also at about **$1.72M to $1.78M** in its own detailed proposal.

This should be reconciled line by line.

### 2. Mobilization is dramatically different
Advanced Roofing’s mobilization on the ACG bid tab is **$548,665.84**, while CARE and JK are only **$15,000 to $41,500**.

That is such a large difference that it demands explanation.

### 3. Scope may not be identical across documents
The documents do not appear to be perfectly standardized. Possible differences include:

- quantity takeoffs
- main roof square footage
- hip roof inclusion or treatment
- flashing and drainage scope
- insulation layers
- alternates versus base scope
- structural or concrete repair assumptions

### 4. Some line items appear inconsistent or flagged
In the Advanced documents, some quantities appear to be marked as not matching plan quantities, or are tied to RFI responses and not fully included in the base bid.

This creates uncertainty about the real final price.

### 5. Price spread is unusually large
CARE and JK are generally in a competitive range around **$900K to $1.14M**.  
Advanced is much higher in its standalone proposal at **$1.72M to $1.78M**.

That spread is large enough to raise questions about whether the board is comparing equivalent scopes.

## Plain-English Explanation for Owners

Residents should understand that the roof replacement documents do **not** tell a simple or consistent story.

Two contractors, CARE and JK, seem to fall in a similar range of roughly **$900,000 to $1.14 million**.

Advanced Roofing, however, appears in two different ways:

- around **$999,000** on the engineer’s bid sheet
- around **$1.72 million to $1.78 million** in its own detailed proposal

That is a major discrepancy.

Before any contract is approved or defended, the association should provide a clear written reconciliation showing:

1. which Advanced Roofing proposal is being relied upon
2. whether all bids include the same scope
3. why Advanced’s mobilization is so high in the comparison sheet
4. whether tile roof work, drainage, flashing, electrical, and repair assumptions are identical across all bidders

Without that clarification, owners cannot tell whether they are looking at true apples-to-apples pricing.
