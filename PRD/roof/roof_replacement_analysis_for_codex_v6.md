## What "Mobilization" Means

In construction contracts, mobilization generally refers to the costs required to start and organize the project before production work begins.

Typical mobilization items can include:

- equipment delivery
- lifts, dumpsters, and staging
- temporary fencing or storage
- project setup logistics
- initial supervision and safety setup

Contractors sometimes structure these costs differently, so mobilization amounts are not always directly comparable unless a bid specification requires identical treatment.

## Why Totals May Differ

Several factors can explain differences between totals shown in different documents.

### 1. Different document formats
Engineer bid-tab sheets summarize bids differently than detailed contractor proposals.

### 2. Scope organization differences
Roof work components such as flat roof areas, tile sections, flashing, insulation, and drainage may be grouped differently.

### 3. Cost allocation methods
Contractors may distribute costs differently among mobilization, supervision, overhead, or other categories.

### 4. Bond, electrical, or alternates
Some totals include these items while others list them separately.

### 5. Quantity or scope assumptions
The posted documents do not explicitly confirm that all values are based on identical quantity assumptions.
A reconciliation document would clarify this.
