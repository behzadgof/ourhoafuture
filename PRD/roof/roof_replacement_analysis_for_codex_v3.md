# What Mobilization Means

In construction contracts, **mobilization** typically refers to costs required to start the project and prepare the site for work. Examples include:

- equipment delivery
- staging areas and dumpsters
- lifts and temporary protection
- temporary fencing or storage
- project startup logistics
- initial supervision and safety setup

Because contractors sometimes organize costs differently, mobilization values are not always directly comparable unless the bidding instructions required a uniform definition.

# Why the Totals May Differ

The following factors may explain differences in totals across documents.

### 1. Different document formats
A one-page engineer bid tab may present costs differently than a contractor’s detailed proposal.

### 2. Scope organization differences
Flat roof work, hip roof work, flashing, drainage, insulation, and alternates may be grouped differently across proposals.

### 3. Cost allocation differences
Contractors may allocate cost into mobilization, overhead, supervision, or general conditions differently.

### 4. Inclusion of bond, electrical, or alternates
Some totals include these items while others show them separately.

### 5. Quantity or scope assumptions
The posted documents do not explicitly confirm identical quantity assumptions across all formats. A reconciliation would clarify this.
