Financial Transparency & Unanswered Questions (2025)
Purpose

This page highlights key financial issues and inconsistencies identified in the 2025 financial reports issued by the association.

The goal is not to make accusations, but to help residents understand:

areas where financial results differed significantly from the approved budget

items in the statements that were flagged for reconciliation or audit review

questions that residents may reasonably ask management or the board

All observations below are based solely on information contained in the association’s monthly financial reports.

Issue 1 – Operating Deficit

The association ended 2025 with a net operating loss.

Category	Amount
Total Operating Income	$5,634,754
Total Operating Expenses	$5,812,294
Operating Result	-$177,539

This indicates that operating expenses exceeded operating income during the year.

Why this matters

A persistent operating deficit can eventually require:

higher assessments

reduced services

or use of reserves if deficits continue.

Issue 2 – Administrative Spending More Than Doubled

Administrative costs significantly exceeded the approved budget.

Category	Budget	Actual	Difference
Administrative Expenses	$192,064	$409,526	+$217,462

This represents more than a 113% increase above the planned budget.

Key contributors

Administrative expenses include:

legal services

professional services

consulting

administrative overhead

office expenses

Residents may wish to understand which specific services drove this increase.

Issue 3 – Legal Costs More Than Double Budget

Legal expenses were a major driver of administrative cost overruns.

Metric	Amount
Legal Budget	$100,000
Actual Legal Expenses	$208,857
Over Budget	+$108,857

Legal spending exceeded the approved budget by more than 100%.

Questions residents may ask

What issues generated these legal costs?

Were these costs anticipated when the budget was approved?

What steps are being taken to manage legal expenses going forward?

Issue 4 – Utilities Exceeded Budget

Utility costs were significantly higher than projected.

Category	Budget	Actual	Difference
Utilities	$529,366	$605,694	+$76,328

This includes:

electricity

natural gas

water and sewer

Potential factors

Utility increases may reflect:

rising energy costs

usage increases

pricing adjustments from service providers

Understanding the cause helps determine whether cost-control measures may be possible.

Issue 5 – Early Financial Statements Flagged for Audit

Several early-year financial reports included important notes regarding the reliability of the data.

Statements indicated:

financial statements were unaudited

a forensic audit review was underway

certain accounts were pending reconciliation

some bank statements were not yet available

Why this matters

When financial statements contain reconciliation notes, the figures may later be adjusted once reconciliation is completed.

Residents should understand whether any adjustments were made after the audit process.

Issue 6 – Large Fluctuations in Receivable Balances

Receivable balances changed significantly during the year.

Large changes in receivables can result from:

collection activity

accounting reclassifications

timing differences in assessment payments

adjustments to prior balances

Understanding these changes helps explain cash flow movements during the year.

Issue 7 – Internal Transfers Between Funds

Monthly balance sheets included entries labeled:

Due (to)/from Reserve Fund
Due (to)/from Operating

These entries represent internal accounting transfers between operating funds and reserve funds.

Why this matters

Internal transfers may occur for legitimate accounting purposes, such as:

correcting timing differences

recording shared expenses

reallocating funds between accounts

Residents may wish to understand the purpose of these transfers and how they were approved.

Issue 8 – Cash Balances Increased During the Year

Despite the operating deficit, total cash balances increased.

Month	Total Cash
March 2025	$3.5M
June 2025	$3.8M
August 2025	$4.7M
November 2025	$5.1M
December 2025	$5.18M

Cash balances can increase even during an operating deficit due to:

reserve assessments

collections of receivables

timing differences between income and expenses

Understanding these movements requires reviewing both income statements and balance sheets together.

Issue 9 – Reserve Fund Increased During 2025

The deferred capital replacement fund increased during the year.

Month	Reserve Fund
March 2025	$1.48M
June 2025	$1.79M
August 2025	$2.00M
November 2025	$2.86M
December 2025	$2.96M

This indicates that reserves were not depleted during 2025.

Questions Residents May Consider

Residents reviewing the financial statements may wish to ask management or the board:

What factors caused administrative spending to exceed budget by more than $217,000?

What legal matters generated more than $200,000 in legal expenses?

Were any financial statement adjustments made after the forensic review?

What explains the significant fluctuations in receivable balances during the year?

How will future budgets address the operating deficit?

Commitment to Financial Transparency

Healthy associations benefit from:

clear financial reporting

accurate reconciliations

strong budget controls

open communication with residents

Transparent financial reporting helps ensure confidence in the management of community funds.
