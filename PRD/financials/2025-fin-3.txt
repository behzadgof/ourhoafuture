Purpose

This page explains how the association spent money during 2025, where spending exceeded the approved budget, and which categories contributed most to the operational deficit.

All numbers are derived from the monthly financial reports issued by the association.

Financial Warning Indicators

The financial statements contain several warning signs that deserve attention.

Administrative Cost Escalation

Administrative spending more than doubled the approved budget.

Legal Expense Growth

Legal expenses exceeded the budget by over $108,000.

Utility Overruns

Utilities exceeded projections by over $76,000.

Accounting Irregularities

What Residents Should Monitor

Residents should monitor the following areas in future financial reports.

Administrative Spending

Large deviations from budget should be explained.

Legal Costs

Legal expenses should be reviewed and controlled.

Utility Efficiency

Energy and water usage should be evaluated for cost savings.

Budget Controls

Budget variance monitoring should be improved.

Key Takeaway

The financial statements show that:

Operating expenses exceeded income in 2025

Several major expense categories exceeded budget

Administrative and legal costs were the largest contributors

Total cash and reserve balances increased despite the deficit

The primary financial issue is operational spending discipline rather than reserve depletion.
