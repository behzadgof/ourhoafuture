Turnberry on the Green
2025 Operational Financial Analysis
Overview

The 2025 financial statements indicate that the association experienced a significant operational deficit, driven primarily by administrative overruns, legal expenses, utilities, insurance increases, and repair costs.

While total cash balances increased during the year, the operating budget itself was not sustainable, meaning operating expenses exceeded operating income.

The December 2025 statement reports:

Total Income: $5,634,754.61

Total Expenses: $5,812,294.01

Operating Net Loss: -$177,539.40

This indicates that operations required funding support from cash balances rather than operating income.

Key Operational Concerns
1. Structural Operating Deficit

Operating expenses exceeded income by $177,539.

2. Administrative Overspending

Administrative spending exceeded budget by $217,462, primarily due to legal expenses.

3. High Legal Costs

Legal expenses were more than double the annual budget.

4. Rising Utility Costs

Utilities exceeded projections by $76,328.

5. Accounting Irregularities

Early financial statements noted:

Missing bank reconciliation

Pending forensic audit

Unreconciled accounts

Large receivable fluctuations
